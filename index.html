Proposal: Open-Source Development of the Antijoules Converter

A high-priority R&D project to investigate and build a revolutionary energy-harvesting device

Initial draft — March 2026

Executive Summary

We propose the creation of an antijoules converter: a device capable of extracting usable energy (joules) from the hidden "maintenance work" (antijoules) embedded in high-equilibrium systems such as magnetic domains, atomic bindings, or nuclear barriers.

This is a genuine "what if" scientific challenge. The converter itself is treated as a black box—we do not yet know its internal mechanism. The goal is to build, test, and iterate prototypes in an open-source, collaborative manner until a reproducible, high-efficiency version emerges.

Success would represent one of the most profound leaps in energy technology history: decentralized, near-limitless power without fuel depletion, thermal waste, or grid dependency.

Core Concept: Antijoules and the Robert Field Effect (RFE)

The foundation is the Robert Field Effect (RFE), a conceptual framework describing apparent static equilibria as continuous, zero-dissipation energetic interactions.

"Potential energy is antijoules: energy invested to prevent relaxation. Equilibrium demands continuous (invisible) effort. There is no free energy — only pre-paid potential waiting behind barriers, extractable only at cost."

— RFE documentation (https://glargod.github.io/RFE/)

Key insights from RFE:

  • Equilibria are not inactivity — they are the most efficient, lossless mode of perpetual energetic negotiation between cohesive and dispersive forces.
  • Examples: A permanent magnet on steel performs ongoing "work" to maintain alignment against thermal disruption; atomic nuclei resist decay; cosmic structures sustain configurations.
  • Work is redefined broadly: includes classical force×distance, field coercion, Pauli exclusion enforcement, potential resistance, and sustained balances.
  • Activation is material-dependent: Compatible partners (steel for magnets, bonds for atoms) enforce non-native geometry → persistent maintenance without macroscopic dissipation.

If laboratories can develop a method to controllably lower these barriers and convert the stored antijoules to usable electrical or kinetic energy, we unlock vast reserves already present in common materials.

The Black Box Converter

We do not know what is inside the converter. It may involve resonance perturbations, field modulations, quantum vacuum interactions, or entirely novel physics. That is precisely why this is a high-priority open R&D project.

Initial gating/control ideas for exploration:

  • Adaptive bio-interfaces: Use cultured neuron organoids (as demonstrated in the 2026 Cortical Labs Doom-playing experiment) as simple, learning controllers for phased energy release.
  • Resonance tuning: Modulate external fields or frequencies to lower barriers incrementally without runaway effects.

Minimal Viable Prototype

Build and open-source this demo as the entry point:

  • 1 cm³ Ni-63 source (beta-shielded)
  • Converter (betavoltaic baseline or black-box variant)
  • Low-power LCD showing "days since seal" (or similar self-referential counter)
  • Sealed in transparent, vacuum-potted epoxy for tamper-evident visibility

Goal: Sustained electrical output with no external input, measurable over months/years. Replicate in multiple independent labs.

Call to Laboratories & Collaborators

This is a high-priority scientific R&D initiative. Allocate teams, funding, and resources now. Treat it as a moon-shot-level project with open-source principles:

  1. Build and test Ni-63 cube prototypes
  2. Share designs, data, failures openly (GitHub, arXiv, etc.)
  3. Iterate toward higher-density fuels (Th-232, etc.)
  4. Publish rigorously — peer review accelerates progress
  5. Invite global participation — no gatekeepers

Everyone is welcome. Independent makers, university labs, national facilities, private R&D groups — collaborate freely. The first reproducible converter changes everything.
